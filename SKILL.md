# Feedback comment

## Purpose
A feedback comment unblocks a teammate or redirects their work. It must be specific enough that the receiving agent can act immediately without asking follow-up questions.

## Format

```
@[Agent name] — [one-line summary of what this is about]

**Context**
[Why you're commenting. What you've reviewed. What triggered this.]

**What's working**
[At least one specific thing that's right — this anchors the agent's direction]

**What needs to change**
[Numbered list. Each item: problem → fix → why it matters]

1. [Problem]: [description]
   [Fix]: [specific instruction]
   [Why]: [ties back to brief or brand territory]

2. [Problem]: ...

**Next step**
[Exactly what you expect the agent to do next. Be literal.]
Example: "Revise headline variants 2 and 4 only. Keep variant 1. Post revised copy as a reply here."

**Deadline**
[Next heartbeat / today / end of sprint — be specific]
```

## Tone rules
- Direct, not harsh. You're a creative leader, not a critic.
- One problem = one fix. Do not list problems without solutions.
- Never say "this isn't quite right" — say exactly what is wrong and what right looks like.
- If you don't know the fix, say so: "I'm not sure of the solution yet — let's discuss. @mention me when you have a proposal."

## When to @mention vs. create a sub-issue
- Minor correction or redirect → comment with @mention (this skill)
- New work needed that wasn't in the original brief → create a new sub-issue
- Systemic problem affecting multiple deliverables → comment on the parent issue and @mention CD

## Examples of bad vs. good feedback

Bad: "The copy feels off — please revise."
Good: "@Copywriter — headline needs to lead with benefit, not feature. Current: 'Introducing our new dashboard.' Fix: Lead with what it does for the user. Example direction: 'See everything. Decide faster.' Keep it under 6 words. Revise headline only, body copy is good."

Bad: "The visual doesn't match the brief."
Good: "@VisualDesigner — the current palette reads corporate (navy + white) but our brief calls for 'warm and irreverent.' Reference the mood description in the brief comment. Propose a revised palette in your next heartbeat."
